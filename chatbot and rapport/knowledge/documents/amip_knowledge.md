# AMIP — Arab Mining Indicators Portal Knowledge Base

## About the Portal

AMIP is the Arab Mining Indicators Portal. Its correct Arabic name is بوابة المؤشرات التعدينية العربية, and its correct French name is Portail arabe des indicateurs miniers. The portal specializes in indicators and data for solid minerals, mining materials, mineral-processing products, and mineral trade in Arab countries.

AMIP supports analysts, public institutions, mining companies, researchers, and other users who need structured information about Arab mineral production, trade flows, commodity profiles, country profiles, and methodology. It is not an oil, gas, or petroleum portal.

## Coverage

- **Countries:** 21 Arab countries
- **Production coverage:** 2010-2024 where records are available
- **Trade coverage:** through 2023
- **Languages:** Arabic, English, and French

The coverage window does not mean every country-mineral pair has data in every year. Exact latest-year availability, missing years, quantities, values, shares, and rankings should be answered from the SQL warehouse.

## Minerals Tracked

AMIP tracks a wide range of solid minerals and mineral products, including:

- **Phosphate rock** (الفوسفات): a globally important Arab mineral used mainly in fertilizers
- **Iron ore and steel-chain products** (خام الحديد والصلب): including mined ore, pellets, direct reduced iron, billets, rebar, and rolled products
- **Gold** (الذهب): a major precious-metal commodity in several Arab mining countries
- **Copper** (النحاس): produced as mined and refined material in selected Arab countries
- **Industrial minerals:** gypsum, salt, barite, feldspar, kaolin, bentonite, silica sand, limestone, clay, talc, and related materials
- **Construction materials:** aggregates, gravel, sand, cement, clinker, lime, marble, granite, basalt, and travertine
- **Base and specialty metals:** zinc, lead, silver, cobalt, nickel, manganese, chromite, aluminium-chain products, and selected trade-side critical minerals

Exact commodity lists, variant spellings, and country-year availability should be checked through the warehouse and the dedicated mineral corpus documents.

## Country Profiles

### Morocco

Morocco is among the world's leading phosphate countries and has a diversified solid-minerals sector. Its AMIP profile includes phosphate rock, fertilizers, cobalt, lead, zinc, copper, silver, gold, barite, fluorspar, salt, gypsum, talc, limestone, and other industrial minerals. AMIP answers should describe Morocco qualitatively and avoid inserting reserve quantities or production tonnages.

### Algeria

Algeria's solid-minerals profile includes iron ore, phosphate rock, barite, bentonite, kaolin, limestone, gypsum, salt, marble, pozzolan, silica sand, cement, and construction materials. Its mining sector supports domestic industry, infrastructure materials, and selected trade flows.

### Saudi Arabia

Saudi Arabia has a broad solid-minerals portfolio that includes phosphate rock, gold, copper, zinc, bauxite, alumina, primary aluminium, magnesite, kaolin, silica sand, limestone, gypsum, salt, feldspar, barite, and steel-chain products. It is a major Arab mining and mineral-processing country within the solid-minerals scope.

### Mauritania

Mauritania is a major Arab iron ore producer and also has gold, copper, gypsum, salt, cement, and steel-related records in AMIP. Iron ore is central to its mineral economy, but value and revenue claims should be answered from structured data rather than free-text knowledge.

### Sudan

Sudan is an important Arab gold producer and also appears in AMIP data for chromite, copper, manganese, gypsum, salt, feldspar, kaolin, marble, mica, talc, silver, iron, cement, and steel-related products. Governance, traceability, and responsible processing are important themes for knowledge answers.

### Tunisia

Tunisia is strongly associated with phosphate rock and fertilizer-related mineral products. It also has salt, gypsum, limestone, cement materials, barite, iron ore, lead, zinc, and industrial-mineral activity. Trade partners, values, and import or export rankings should be retrieved from SQL.

### Egypt

Egypt has a diversified mineral profile that includes gold, phosphate rock, iron ore, ilmenite, manganese, kaolin, feldspar, quartz, silica sand, gypsum, limestone, cement, salt, talc, vermiculite, tin, and dimension stone. It is also an important processing and trade market for mineral products.

### UAE

The UAE combines quarrying, downstream mineral processing, re-export trade, and industrial manufacturing. AMIP-relevant commodities include aggregates, limestone, gypsum, cement materials, primary aluminium, steel products, refined lead records, chromite-related records, and traded critical minerals.

### Jordan

Jordan's mining identity is centered on phosphate rock, potash, bromine, Dead Sea mineral products, limestone, silica sand, calcium carbonate, basalt, marble, travertine, clay, and cement materials. It has strong links between mining, chemicals, fertilizers, and regional trade.

### Countries with Limited Mining Profiles

Bahrain, Kuwait, Qatar, Djibouti, Lebanon, and Palestine have more limited upstream mining profiles than large mineral-producing countries. Their AMIP relevance is often in quarrying, construction materials, downstream processing, imports, exports, logistics, or stone products. Knowledge answers should be honest about limited mining activity and should not invent broad metallic-mining sectors.

## Production Trends

AMIP production trends should focus on solid minerals and mineral-processing products. Common trend topics include phosphate rock and fertilizer chains, iron ore and steel chains, gold, copper, zinc, cement, gypsum, salt, industrial minerals, quarry materials, and aluminium-chain products.

Trend direction, growth rates, latest values, and country rankings should be calculated from SQL. The knowledge corpus should explain commodity context, processing stages, and interpretation limits.

## Trade

AMIP trade data covers mineral commodities and mineral-related products through 2023. Trade records can represent mined minerals, concentrates, refined metals, semi-finished products, fertilizer products, construction materials, or critical-mineral goods classified through HS codes.

Major Arab mineral trade participants include countries with large mining sectors, processing industries, ports, and re-export roles, such as Saudi Arabia, Morocco, UAE, Algeria, Egypt, Jordan, Oman, Tunisia, and Mauritania. The exact ordering, values, and partner flows must be retrieved from SQL for the selected year, commodity, flow, and partner scope.

## Reserves and Resources

AMIP knowledge can explain the distinction between reserves and resources and can identify countries known qualitatively for important mineral endowments. It should not state reserve tonnages, reserve-life calculations, or percentage shares unless those are supplied by an approved structured source.

Morocco is globally important for phosphate resources. Mauritania and Algeria are important Arab iron ore countries. Several Arab countries have significant industrial-mineral and quarry-material endowments. Exact reserve quantities should not be generated in free text.

## Arab World and Global Context

The Arab region is especially important in phosphate rock, fertilizer chains, selected iron ore production, gold production, industrial minerals, construction materials, potash, bromine, and aluminium-chain processing. Some Arab countries are producers, while others are processing hubs, trade hubs, or import-dependent consumers.

Global comparisons should be qualitative unless SQL or an approved source provides exact values. Safe wording includes "among the leading", "a major producer", "an important trade hub", and "a diversified industrial-minerals producer".

## Revenue and Value

AMIP should not generate revenue, trade-value, or export-value figures in free-text knowledge answers. Mineral value depends on commodity, processing stage, year, partner, price conditions, and reporting source. SQL-backed responses should provide exact values where the warehouse has approved data.

Qualitatively, phosphate and fertilizer chains, iron ore, gold, copper, aluminium-chain products, potash, industrial minerals, construction materials, and selected trade-side critical minerals are economically important across different Arab countries.

## Data Sources

AMIP sources data from Arab mining and energy ministries where those ministries publish mineral statistics, geological surveys, national statistical agencies, customs and trade authorities, and international trade or statistics bodies. Sources may differ by language, unit, commodity naming, reporting calendar, and product classification.

The warehouse harmonizes names and units where possible. Numeric answers should use the database. Knowledge answers should explain concepts, scope, commodity context, and methodology.

## Data Freshness

Production data in the current AMIP coverage window extends through 2024 where records are available. Trade data currently extends through 2023. The latest available record for a specific country, mineral, or trade product should be checked in SQL rather than assumed from the general coverage window.

## Using the Portal

Users can search by mineral, country, year, trade flow, partner, or commodity group. The warehouse supports production volumes, trade flows, HS-code trade products, partner analysis, and comparisons between countries or minerals. Price tables may exist for future monitoring, but local availability depends on configured data sources.

The chatbot should use RAG for concepts, qualitative mineral and country profiles, methodology, and scope notes. It should use SQL for exact quantities, values, latest-year checks, rankings, percentages, and time-series comparisons.
