# Mineral Commodity Profiles

These profiles support the Arab Mining Indicators Portal knowledge corpus. They describe solid minerals, quarry materials, mineral-processing products, and trade-side critical minerals in qualitative terms. AMIP production coverage spans 2010-2024, and mineral trade coverage currently runs through 2023.

## Aggregates, Gravel, Construction Sand, and Building Materials

Variant forms covered: aggregates and gravel, gravel, sand and gravel, construction sand, and building materials.

Aggregates are naturally occurring or crushed rock materials used in concrete, asphalt, road bases, drainage layers, and general civil works. They are usually quarried from limestone, basalt, gabbro, sandstone, or alluvial deposits, then crushed, washed, screened, and graded to construction specifications. Construction sand is separated from industrial silica sand because its value lies mainly in particle size, cleanliness, and suitability for mortar, concrete, and earthworks rather than chemical purity. Economically, aggregates are local, bulky, and infrastructure-driven: they support housing, roads, ports, and industrial zones more than long-distance mineral trade. Notable Arab producers: Algeria, Saudi Arabia, Oman, UAE, Egypt, Yemen.

## Aluminium, Bauxite, and Alumina

Variant forms covered: bauxite, low-percentage bauxite, alumina, and primary aluminium.

Bauxite is the principal ore used to make alumina, which is then smelted into primary aluminium. Low-percentage bauxite describes lower-grade material that may need blending or beneficiation before it can feed an alumina refinery. Aluminium is lightweight, corrosion-resistant, and widely used in transport, packaging, electrical systems, construction, machinery, and renewable-energy components. The mining chain begins with bauxite extraction, crushing, washing, and refining into alumina; the downstream chain uses energy-intensive smelting and casting. In Arab economies, aluminium is especially important where bauxite mining, alumina refining, smelting, and port logistics are integrated with industrial diversification. Notable Arab producers: Saudi Arabia, UAE, Bahrain, Qatar, Oman, Egypt.

## Ammonia and Mineral Fertilizers

Variant forms covered: ammonia and fertilizers.

Ammonia is a chemical feedstock for nitrogen fertilizers, while mineral fertilizers in AMIP trade data include processed nutrient products linked to phosphate, potash, sulfur, and nitrogen chemistry. This profile is included because fertilizer products are an important downstream expression of the solid-minerals value chain, especially where phosphate rock and potash are beneficiated or chemically processed. Fertilizers are used to replenish crop nutrients, improve yields, and support food security. Processing can include beneficiation of mineral feedstocks, acidulation, granulation, blending, and packaging. Economically, fertilizers connect mining with agriculture, ports, bulk logistics, and regional trade. Notable Arab producers: Morocco, Saudi Arabia, Jordan, Tunisia, Egypt, Qatar, Oman, Algeria.

## Arsenic

Variant forms covered: arsenic.

Arsenic is a metalloid that commonly occurs as a trace constituent in complex sulfide ores rather than as a stand-alone mining target. It can be associated with copper, gold, lead, zinc, and cobalt mineralization, and it requires careful environmental control during mining, concentration, smelting, and tailings management. Modern uses are specialized and regulated, including selected alloy, semiconductor, wood-treatment legacy, and chemical applications, but its main relevance for mining knowledge is often as an impurity that affects concentrate quality and processing routes. Economically, arsenic is important because it can influence ore valuation, treatment charges, and environmental compliance. Notable Arab producers: Morocco.

## Barite

Variant forms covered: barytes and barite.

Barite is a dense barium sulfate mineral used mainly as a weighting agent in drilling muds, as well as in paints, plastics, rubber, radiation shielding, and selected chemical products. The correct commodity name is barite or barytes; both terms refer to the same mineral, and the corpus should normalize them together. Mining is commonly by open-pit methods, followed by crushing, jigging, flotation, magnetic separation, or grinding to meet density and purity specifications. Barite has economic importance because it links industrial minerals with drilling services, construction materials, and chemical manufacturing, while high-density grades can support export trade where logistics are favorable. Notable Arab producers: Morocco, Algeria, Tunisia, Egypt, Saudi Arabia, Somalia.

## Basalt

Variant forms covered: basalt.

Basalt is a dark volcanic rock valued as crushed stone, armour stone, railway ballast, road aggregate, concrete aggregate, and, in some markets, fiber or cast-stone feedstock. It is generally quarried from lava flows or volcanic outcrops, then blasted, crushed, screened, and sized. Its hardness and durability make it useful in infrastructure, coastal protection, and high-wear construction settings. Basalt is normally a regional commodity because transport costs matter, but high-quality stone can serve specialized construction and industrial uses. In the Arab region, basalt also matters geologically because it is common in volcanic terrains and can coexist with pozzolan, tuff, scoria, and building-stone resources. Notable Arab producers: Saudi Arabia, Jordan, Yemen.

## Bentonite

Variant forms covered: bentonite.

Bentonite is an absorbent clay dominated by smectite minerals. It swells in water and is valued for binding, sealing, suspension, and adsorption properties. Major applications include foundry sands, drilling muds, civil-engineering barriers, pelletizing binders, animal feed additives, cat litter, clarification, and environmental liners. Mining is typically shallow quarrying, followed by drying, crushing, activation where needed, grinding, and quality classification by swelling capacity, viscosity, and purity. Bentonite supports both local industry and export trade because small differences in chemistry and performance affect market value. In Arab mining, it is part of the wider industrial-clays family alongside kaolin, common clay, and pyrophyllite. Notable Arab producers: Morocco, Algeria, Egypt, Iraq.

## Bromine and Dead Sea Mud

Variant forms covered: bromine and Dead Sea mud.

Bromine is produced from mineral-rich brines, especially where evaporite basins concentrate dissolved salts. It is used in flame retardants, drilling fluids, water treatment, pharmaceuticals, specialty chemicals, and some energy-storage applications. Dead Sea mud is a mineral-rich sediment used mainly in wellness, cosmetics, and therapeutic products rather than heavy industry. Both belong to the broader Dead Sea mineral economy, but they serve very different markets: bromine is a chemical raw material, while mud is a consumer and health-oriented mineral product. Processing involves brine extraction and chemical separation for bromine, and controlled collection, cleaning, and packaging for mud. Notable Arab producers: Jordan.

## Carbonate Minerals: Limestone, Calcium Carbonate, Dolomite, and Lime

Variant forms covered: calcium carbonate, limestone, limestone blocks, pure limestone, dolomite, industrial dolomite, lime, and lime for cement.

Carbonate minerals are among the most widely used industrial and construction materials. Limestone and calcium carbonate feed cement, lime, glass, paper, plastics, paints, agriculture, water treatment, and building stone markets. Dolomite supplies magnesium-bearing carbonate for glass, steel fluxes, refractories, agriculture, and aggregate. Lime is produced by calcining limestone and is used in metallurgy, environmental treatment, construction, sugar refining, and cement-related processes. Mining is usually quarry-based, with crushing, screening, washing, calcination, milling, or block cutting depending on the product. Carbonates are economically significant because they anchor cement plants, building-material clusters, and local industrial supply chains. Notable Arab producers: Algeria, Jordan, Lebanon, Oman, Qatar, Saudi Arabia, UAE, Yemen.

## Cement and Clinker

Variant forms covered: cement, gray Portland cement, white cement, and clinker.

Cement is a manufactured mineral product made by grinding clinker with gypsum and other constituents. Clinker is produced by heating limestone-rich raw meal with clay or corrective minerals in a kiln. Gray Portland cement is the standard construction binder, while white cement uses carefully selected low-iron raw materials for architectural and decorative uses. Cement underpins housing, infrastructure, industrial construction, and reconstruction activity, so it is a strategic domestic commodity even when it is not a high-value export. The chain depends on limestone, clay, gypsum, energy, grinding capacity, packaging, and bulk transport. Notable Arab producers: Egypt, Saudi Arabia, Algeria, Morocco, Tunisia, Jordan, Iraq, Sudan, Lebanon, Yemen, Mauritania, Djibouti.

## Chromite and Chromium

Variant forms covered: chromite ore and chromium.

Chromite is the main ore of chromium, a metal valued for hardness, corrosion resistance, and its role in stainless steel and alloy production. Chromite ore can be mined from layered intrusions or podiform deposits and is commonly crushed, screened, gravity concentrated, and sometimes upgraded before smelting. Chromium also appears in industrial chemicals, refractories, pigments, and ferrochromium, but the largest strategic link is to steelmaking. For Arab producers, chromite can be a small but important export mineral where ore quality, access to ports, and metallurgical demand align. Processing and marketing depend strongly on chrome grade, iron ratio, and impurity control. Notable Arab producers: Oman, Sudan, UAE.

## Clay and Kaolin

Variant forms covered: clay, kaolin, sandy kaolin, and common clay.

Clay is a broad family of fine-grained minerals used in bricks, tiles, cement raw mix, ceramics, liners, absorbents, and construction materials. Kaolin is a higher-value white clay used in ceramics, paper coating and filling, paint, rubber, plastics, fiberglass, refractories, and cosmetics. Mining is usually shallow and selective, followed by blunging, screening, washing, magnetic separation, drying, and milling when higher purity is required. Clay resources are economically important because they serve both local construction and higher-grade industrial markets. Distinguishing common clay from kaolin matters in AMIP retrieval because their uses, processing intensity, and trade patterns differ. Notable Arab producers: Egypt, Saudi Arabia, Algeria, Iraq, Sudan, Oman, Jordan.

## Cobalt

Variant forms covered: cobalt mine production.

Cobalt is a strategic metal used in rechargeable batteries, superalloys, hard metals, catalysts, pigments, magnets, and specialty chemicals. It is usually produced as a by-product or co-product of copper, nickel, or complex polymetallic ores rather than from a simple stand-alone cobalt mine. Mining and processing require selective flotation, hydrometallurgy, refining, and strict impurity control because battery and alloy uses demand consistent chemistry. Cobalt is economically significant because it connects traditional mining with energy-transition supply chains and high-specification manufacturing. In Arab mineral contexts, cobalt is notable where polymetallic deposits and refining knowledge create specialty-metal opportunities. Notable Arab producers: Morocco.

## Copper

Variant forms covered: copper mine production, refined copper, and copper slices and molds.

Copper is a conductive, ductile metal essential to electrical wiring, power grids, electronics, motors, renewable energy systems, plumbing, alloys, and industrial machinery. Mine production includes ore extraction and concentration, while refined copper comes from smelting, electrorefining, or electrowinning. Semi-finished copper forms such as slices, molds, billets, or cast products belong to downstream metalworking rather than ore mining. Copper deposits may be volcanic massive sulfide, porphyry, sediment-hosted, or vein-related, and processing depends on sulfide or oxide mineralogy. Economically, copper is a benchmark industrial metal and a useful indicator of manufacturing and infrastructure demand. Notable Arab producers: Morocco, Saudi Arabia, Oman, Mauritania, Sudan, Egypt.

## Diatomite

Variant forms covered: diatomite.

Diatomite is a light, porous sedimentary rock made from fossilized diatom shells. Its high porosity, low density, and silica-rich composition make it useful in filtration, absorbents, fillers, insulation, agriculture, paints, plastics, and specialty construction products. Mining is normally from soft sedimentary deposits, followed by drying, crushing, sizing, calcination for some grades, and careful classification to preserve performance. Diatomite can be valuable even in small deposits because industrial buyers select material by filtration speed, brightness, density, and chemical purity. In Arab industrial minerals, it complements silica, clay, pozzolan, and lightweight aggregate resources. Notable Arab producers: Algeria, Saudi Arabia.

## Dimension Stone: Granite, Marble, and Travertine

Variant forms covered: granite, marble, marble blocks, marble for industrial purposes, limestone blocks, and travertine.

Dimension stone is quarried in blocks or slabs for architectural, decorative, structural, and memorial uses. Granite is valued for hardness and polish, marble for appearance and workability, travertine for textured carbonate surfaces, and selected limestone blocks for building and cladding. Marble can also be crushed or milled for industrial fillers when block quality is unsuitable. Processing includes wire-saw cutting, drilling, splitting, trimming, slab sawing, polishing, resin treatment, and tile production. Economic value depends on color, pattern, fracture spacing, block size, finishing quality, and access to export logistics. In Arab countries, stone quarrying is often closely tied to construction, heritage architecture, and small and medium enterprises. Notable Arab producers: Jordan, Saudi Arabia, Oman, Sudan, Yemen, Algeria, Egypt, Palestine.

## Feldspar

Variant forms covered: feldspar, vein feldspar, alluvial feldspar, feldspar with quartz, and feldspar with quartz and mica.

Feldspar is a group of aluminosilicate minerals used mainly in ceramics, glass, sanitaryware, tiles, enamels, fillers, and some abrasives. It acts as a flux, lowering melting temperatures and improving strength and finish in ceramic bodies and glass batches. Feldspar can be mined from pegmatites, veins, granitic rocks, or alluvial deposits, then crushed, ground, magnetically separated, floated, and blended to meet iron, alkali, and particle-size specifications. Variant names in source data often describe the deposit style or associated minerals, especially quartz and mica. Economically, feldspar supports ceramic clusters, glass manufacturing, and export markets for consistent industrial-mineral grades. Notable Arab producers: Egypt, Saudi Arabia, Morocco, Algeria, Sudan.

## Ferro-alloys

Variant forms covered: ferro-manganese, high- or low-carbon ferro-manganese, ferro-silicon, ferro-silicomanganese, ferrochromium, and other ferro-alloys.

Ferro-alloys are iron-bearing alloy products used to introduce manganese, silicon, chromium, and other elements into steel. They are produced in furnaces from ores, reductants, fluxes, and energy, and are classified by alloy chemistry and carbon content. Ferro-manganese and ferro-silicomanganese support deoxidation and manganese addition; ferro-silicon supports deoxidation and silicon addition; ferrochromium is the main chromium input for stainless and alloy steels. These products are processing-stage commodities, not raw ores, and their economics depend on power, ore supply, steel demand, logistics, and furnace efficiency. Notable Arab producers: Saudi Arabia, Egypt, Bahrain, Oman.

## Fluorspar

Variant forms covered: fluorspar and fluorite.

Fluorspar, also called fluorite, is calcium fluoride. The source typo "Flourite" should be normalized to fluorspar or fluorite. It is used to make hydrofluoric acid, aluminium fluoride, refrigerant and specialty chemicals, steelmaking fluxes, cement additives, ceramics, and optical or metallurgical products. Mining can be underground or open-pit, followed by crushing, dense-media separation, flotation, drying, and grading into acid, ceramic, or metallurgical specifications. Fluorspar has strategic value because chemical-grade material feeds industrial chains that are difficult to substitute. In Arab mining, it is part of the broader industrial-minerals portfolio alongside barite, phosphate, silica, and carbonate minerals. Notable Arab producers: Morocco, Egypt, Sudan.

## Gold

Variant forms covered: gold.

Gold is a precious metal valued for jewelry, investment, central-bank holdings, electronics, dentistry, and specialized industrial uses. It occurs in quartz veins, volcanic and sediment-hosted systems, shear zones, placers, and as a by-product in polymetallic deposits. Mining ranges from artisanal and small-scale operations to modern open-pit and underground mines. Processing may include gravity recovery, flotation, leaching, adsorption, refining, and responsible tailings management. Gold is economically significant because it can generate export earnings, attract exploration, and support local service industries, while also requiring strong governance over licensing, mercury avoidance, and traceability. Notable Arab producers: Sudan, Egypt, Saudi Arabia, Mauritania, Morocco, Algeria.

## Gypsum

Variant forms covered: gypsum, initial gypsum, and secondary gypsum.

Gypsum is a soft sulfate mineral used in cement, plaster, wallboard, agriculture, soil conditioning, molds, and specialty construction materials. Initial and secondary gypsum labels describe source or processing-stage distinctions that should be retrieved under the same base commodity. Gypsum is mined from sedimentary evaporite deposits, quarried, crushed, screened, dried, calcined for plaster, or ground for cement retardant and agricultural use. Because it is bulky and widely used, gypsum often serves domestic construction markets, but high-quality material near ports can support regional exports. It is also a key companion to cement production because it helps regulate setting behavior. Notable Arab producers: Oman, Saudi Arabia, Egypt, Algeria, Morocco, Jordan, Sudan, Tunisia, Syria, Lebanon, Yemen, UAE, Mauritania.

## Helium (Scope Boundary)

Variant forms covered: helium.

Helium appears in legacy commodity lists, but it is not a solid mineral and should be treated as a scope boundary for AMIP knowledge retrieval. The Arab Mining Indicators Portal is a solid-minerals portal and should not be described as covering petroleum or general gas production. When a user asks about helium in this corpus, the safe answer is that helium is outside the solid-minerals focus unless it appears only as a source-list label or a trade-classification edge case. Helium itself is used in cryogenics, leak detection, welding atmospheres, balloons, semiconductors, and scientific equipment, but those uses do not make it a mining commodity in AMIP. Notable Arab producers: Qatar.

## Ilmenite

Variant forms covered: ilmenite mine production.

Ilmenite is an iron-titanium oxide mineral and a principal source of titanium feedstock. It is used to produce titanium dioxide pigment for paints, plastics, paper, inks, and coatings, and it can also feed titanium metal and welding-flux industries. Ilmenite may be mined from heavy-mineral sands, hard-rock deposits, or beach and dune systems, then upgraded through gravity, magnetic, and electrostatic separation. Its economic value depends on titanium content, iron chemistry, impurities, grain size, and suitability for slag or synthetic rutile routes. In Arab mineral data, ilmenite is a specialized commodity linked to heavy-mineral exploration and industrial processing. Notable Arab producers: Egypt.

## Iron and Steel

Variant forms covered: iron, iron ore, iron (Fe-content), iron ore pellets, iron sand, iron oxides, iron oxides with manganese, DRI or direct reduced iron, pig iron, steel, billets, rebar, hot rolling, cold rolling, and light steel sections.

Iron ore supplies the steel chain, moving from mined ore to concentrates, pellets, direct reduced iron, pig iron, crude and finished steel, billets, rolled flat products, rebar, and structural sections. Iron oxides may also serve pigments, cement, and metallurgical uses. Mining and processing include drilling, blasting, crushing, screening, beneficiation, pelletizing, reduction, melting, casting, and rolling. Steel is central to construction, machinery, transport, energy infrastructure, and manufacturing, so the chain has high strategic value even when countries participate mainly in processing rather than ore mining. Notable Arab producers: Mauritania, Algeria, Egypt, Saudi Arabia, Morocco, Bahrain, Qatar, UAE, Oman, Libya, Sudan, Tunisia, Kuwait.

## Lead

Variant forms covered: lead, lead mine production, and refined lead.

Lead is a dense, malleable base metal used in batteries, radiation shielding, weights, alloys, cable sheathing, and selected chemical products. It commonly occurs with zinc, silver, copper, and other sulfide minerals, so mining is often polymetallic. Ore processing includes crushing, grinding, flotation, concentrate handling, smelting, and refining; recycling is also important in lead supply chains. Refined lead is a downstream product with stricter purity requirements than mine concentrate. Economically, lead matters because it is tied to zinc and silver districts, battery manufacturing, and environmental management. Responsible handling is essential because lead exposure is hazardous. Notable Arab producers: Morocco, Saudi Arabia, Tunisia, Egypt, UAE.

## Magnesite and Magnesium

Variant forms covered: magnesite and magnesium.

Magnesite is a magnesium carbonate mineral used to produce refractory materials, caustic-calcined magnesia, dead-burned magnesia, fertilizers, environmental products, and specialty chemicals. Magnesium metal is a lightweight material used in alloys, die casting, aerospace components, automotive parts, electronics, and metallurgical applications. Magnesite mining is typically quarry-based, followed by crushing, sorting, calcination, or sintering depending on the market. Magnesium metal requires additional chemical or thermal processing and should be distinguished from raw magnesite in retrieval. The economic significance lies in refractories, steelmaking inputs, light-metal manufacturing, and trade-side critical-mineral supply chains. Notable Arab producers: Saudi Arabia, Egypt.

## Manganese

Variant forms covered: manganese.

Manganese is a key alloying and deoxidizing element in steel and is also used in batteries, chemicals, pigments, fertilizers, and water treatment. It occurs in oxide, carbonate, and silicate minerals and can be mined from sedimentary, supergene, or vein-style deposits. Processing may involve crushing, screening, washing, gravity separation, magnetic separation, and furnace conversion into ferro-manganese or silicomanganese. Manganese has economic importance because steelmaking depends on it and because battery materials have increased interest in higher-purity manganese chemicals. In AMIP knowledge, raw manganese ore and ferro-alloy products should be related but not confused. Notable Arab producers: Morocco, Egypt, Sudan, Oman.

## Mercury

Variant forms covered: mercury.

Mercury is a heavy liquid metal historically produced from cinnabar and used in instruments, chemicals, lamps, switches, and small-scale gold amalgamation. Many uses are now restricted because mercury is toxic and persistent in the environment. In mining knowledge, mercury is important both as a commodity and as a risk factor, especially where legacy operations, artisanal gold activity, or contaminated tailings require careful management. Processing cinnabar involves roasting and condensation, but modern governance focuses on minimizing exposure, controlling emissions, and avoiding mercury-based gold recovery. Its economic role is limited compared with major industrial minerals, yet its environmental relevance is high. Notable Arab producers: Morocco.

## Mica and Muscovite

Variant forms covered: mica and muscovite.

Mica is a group of sheet silicate minerals, and muscovite is one of its most common commercial varieties. Mica is valued for electrical insulation, heat resistance, elasticity, sheen, and platelet shape. Uses include electrical components, paints, plastics, rubber, drilling additives, cosmetics, joint compounds, and specialty insulation. It is commonly recovered from pegmatites, schists, or as a by-product of feldspar and quartz processing. Processing includes hand sorting for sheet mica, crushing, screening, flotation, delamination, and micronizing for ground mica. Economically, mica is a niche industrial mineral whose value depends on purity, particle shape, color, and performance in downstream formulations. Notable Arab producers: Egypt, Sudan.

## Nickel

Variant forms covered: nickel mine production, nickel smelter output, and refined nickel.

Nickel is a corrosion-resistant metal used in stainless steel, superalloys, plating, batteries, catalysts, coins, and specialty chemicals. It is mined from sulfide and laterite deposits, although Arab production references are concentrated in limited polymetallic contexts. Processing can include concentration, smelting, leaching, refining, and production of battery-grade intermediates or metal. The source typo "smellter" should be corrected to smelter. Nickel is economically important because it links stainless steel with energy-storage supply chains, but mine production and refined production are different stages and should be kept distinct. Notable Arab producers: Morocco.

## Phosphate Rock

Variant forms covered: phosphate rock.

Phosphate rock is a sedimentary mineral raw material used mainly to make phosphate fertilizers, phosphoric acid, animal-feed phosphates, and industrial phosphates. It is mined in large open-pit operations or selective deposits, then crushed, screened, washed, floated, calcined, or chemically processed depending on ore quality and product route. Phosphate is one of the Arab region's most globally significant solid-mineral commodities, especially where mining, beneficiation, chemicals, ports, and fertilizer exports are integrated. It supports agriculture, food security, industrial employment, and regional trade. In retrieval, phosphate rock should be connected to fertilizers but not replaced by fertilizer products. Notable Arab producers: Morocco, Saudi Arabia, Jordan, Tunisia, Egypt, Algeria, Iraq, Syria.

## Potash

Variant forms covered: potash.

Potash is a group of potassium-bearing salts used primarily in fertilizers. It improves plant water regulation, stress tolerance, and crop quality. Commercial potash is commonly produced from evaporite deposits or mineral brines through solution mining, solar evaporation, crystallization, flotation, compaction, and granulation. It is closely linked to Dead Sea mineral operations in the Arab region, where brines also support bromine and other chemical products. Potash has strong economic significance because it is a strategic agricultural input and a major traded bulk mineral where quality, logistics, and long-term customer relationships matter. Notable Arab producers: Jordan.

## Pozzolan, Scoria, and Tuff

Variant forms covered: pozzolan, pozzolan and scoria, tuff, volcanic tuff, and volcanic tuff with pozzolan.

Pozzolans are siliceous or aluminosilicate materials that react with lime to form cementitious compounds. Natural pozzolans include volcanic ash, tuff, pumice, and scoria. They are used in blended cement, concrete durability improvement, lightweight aggregates, masonry products, and soil stabilization. Mining is commonly by quarrying volcanic deposits, then crushing, drying, milling, and testing for reactivity. Scoria and tuff can also be used as lightweight aggregate, decorative stone, or road material. Their economic significance is growing where cement producers seek performance, durability, and lower clinker intensity. Notable Arab producers: Saudi Arabia, Algeria, Jordan, Yemen.

## Pyrophyllite

Variant forms covered: pyrophyllite.

Pyrophyllite is a hydrous aluminium silicate mineral used in ceramics, refractories, fillers, paints, rubber, insecticide carriers, sculptural stone, and high-temperature applications. It resembles talc in softness but has different chemistry and firing behavior. Mining is typically selective quarrying or underground extraction from metamorphic deposits, followed by crushing, sorting, grinding, and sometimes beneficiation to control iron, quartz, and accessory minerals. Pyrophyllite's value depends on whiteness, thermal stability, particle size, and low impurity levels. In Arab industrial-mineral portfolios, it is a specialized clay-like commodity rather than a bulk construction material. Notable Arab producers: Saudi Arabia.

## Quartz, Silica, and Silica Sand

Variant forms covered: quartz, silica, silica sand, sand, industrial sand, and quartz with feldspar or mica where listed.

Quartz is crystalline silica, while silica sand is a high-silica granular material used in glass, foundry molds, ceramics, filtration, hydraulic applications, construction chemicals, silicon metal feedstock, and solar-glass supply chains. Ordinary sand can serve construction uses, but industrial silica sand is valued for chemical purity, grain size, brightness, and low iron. Processing includes washing, attrition, screening, magnetic separation, flotation, drying, and sizing. Quartz may also be mined from veins and pegmatites. Economically, silica links quarrying with glass, ceramics, construction products, and critical-material processing. Notable Arab producers: Saudi Arabia, Jordan, Algeria, Iraq, Egypt, Oman, Sudan.

## Salt

Variant forms covered: salt and halite.

Salt, or halite, is sodium chloride produced from rock salt, solar evaporation, lake brines, or seawater. It is used in food, chlor-alkali chemicals, water softening, de-icing where relevant, animal feed, textiles, drilling, leather, and many industrial processes. Production methods include underground mining, solution mining, solar pond evaporation, harvesting, washing, drying, crushing, and grading. Salt is often a local bulk commodity, but high-purity or well-located production can support regional trade. In Arab countries, salt resources are associated with coastal sabkhas, inland basins, evaporite sequences, and arid-climate brines. Notable Arab producers: Egypt, Saudi Arabia, Morocco, Tunisia, Algeria, Sudan, Iraq, Djibouti, Yemen, Syria, Lebanon, Mauritania.

## Schist

Variant forms covered: schist and shale where source labels overlap.

Schist is a foliated metamorphic rock used locally as building stone, decorative stone, aggregate, roofing material in some traditions, and low-grade construction fill. Some source labels may overlap with shale or other platy rocks, so retrieval should keep schist as a construction and dimension-stone material rather than a metallic mineral. Quarrying focuses on bedding, cleavage, durability, color, and block size. Processing is usually simple compared with metallic ores: extraction, splitting, trimming, crushing, or sizing. Its economic importance is local and construction-oriented, especially where transport costs favor nearby stone resources. Notable Arab producers: Saudi Arabia, Algeria.

## Silver

Variant forms covered: silver and silver mine production.

Silver is a precious and industrial metal used in jewelry, investment products, electronics, solar cells, brazing alloys, mirrors, medical applications, and chemical catalysts. It is commonly recovered as a by-product from lead, zinc, copper, and gold ores, although some deposits are silver-rich. Processing routes include flotation, leaching, smelting, refining, and by-product recovery from polymetallic concentrates. Silver's economic significance comes from its dual role as a precious metal and an industrial input, plus its contribution to the value of polymetallic mining districts. In AMIP retrieval, silver should connect to gold and base-metal systems without being treated as the same commodity. Notable Arab producers: Morocco, Saudi Arabia, Sudan, Algeria.

## Sulfur

Variant forms covered: sulfur.

Sulfur is a yellow non-metal used mainly to produce sulfuric acid, which supports phosphate fertilizers, mining leaching, chemicals, water treatment, pulp and paper, rubber, and industrial processing. It can be mined from native sulfur deposits or recovered as a by-product from industrial treatment streams. For AMIP solid-mineral knowledge, sulfur should be described as a mineral and industrial chemical feedstock without expanding the portal beyond solid minerals. Processing and marketing depend on purity, form, storage, and safe transport. Sulfur is economically significant because sulfuric acid is essential to phosphate fertilizer production and many mineral-processing circuits. Notable Arab producers: Saudi Arabia, Egypt, UAE, Kuwait, Qatar, Oman, Bahrain, Libya.

## Talc

Variant forms covered: talc.

Talc is a soft magnesium silicate mineral valued for lubricity, whiteness, chemical inertness, platy particle shape, and heat resistance. It is used in ceramics, paints, plastics, rubber, paper, cosmetics where specifications allow, pharmaceuticals, roofing, insecticide carriers, and specialty fillers. Mining is selective because impurities such as carbonates, chlorite, iron minerals, or asbestos-form minerals can affect suitability. Processing includes sorting, crushing, grinding, air classification, flotation, and micronizing. Talc's economic value depends on brightness, purity, particle size, and compliance with health and product standards. In Arab mineral portfolios, talc is a niche industrial mineral that can support export markets when quality is consistent. Notable Arab producers: Egypt, Morocco, Sudan, Saudi Arabia.

## Tin

Variant forms covered: tin.

Tin is a base metal used in solder, tinplate, bronze, chemicals, glass coatings, bearing metals, and electronics. Its main ore is cassiterite, which is often recovered from alluvial deposits, hard-rock veins, or as a by-product of complex mineral systems. Processing commonly uses gravity concentration, magnetic or electrostatic separation, smelting, and refining. Tin is economically significant because electronics and packaging rely on it, while supply is geographically concentrated worldwide. In Arab production data it appears as a limited commodity, so knowledge answers should be cautious and avoid implying a broad regional tin industry. Notable Arab producers: Egypt.

## Vermiculite

Variant forms covered: vermiculite.

Vermiculite is a hydrated mica-like mineral that expands when heated. Expanded vermiculite is lightweight, absorbent, fire-resistant, and insulating, making it useful in horticulture, lightweight concrete, insulation, fireproofing, packaging, brake linings, and specialty absorbents. Mining is typically open-pit, followed by crushing, screening, beneficiation, exfoliation, and grading by particle size. Product quality depends on expansion ratio, impurity levels, particle distribution, and safety testing. Vermiculite is economically relevant as a specialty industrial mineral with construction, agricultural, and environmental uses rather than as a high-volume metallic commodity. Notable Arab producers: Egypt.

## Zinc

Variant forms covered: zinc mine production, zinc concentrate, refined zinc, and zinc slab.

Zinc is a base metal used mainly for galvanizing steel, die casting, brass, chemicals, batteries, fertilizers, and roofing products. It commonly occurs with lead, silver, copper, and sometimes gold in polymetallic sulfide deposits. Mine production and zinc concentrate describe ore extraction and beneficiation, while refined zinc and zinc slab are smelter products used by manufacturers. Processing includes crushing, grinding, flotation, roasting or pressure leaching, electrowinning, casting, and quality control. Zinc has strong economic significance because galvanizing protects infrastructure from corrosion, and polymetallic deposits can support several metal streams. Notable Arab producers: Morocco, Saudi Arabia, Tunisia, Algeria.

## Lithium and Lithium Cells

Variant forms covered: lithium, lithium cells and batteries, and Li cells/batteries.

Lithium is a light metal used in rechargeable batteries, ceramics, glass, greases, polymers, air treatment, and specialty alloys. Lithium cells and batteries are downstream manufactured products and should be distinguished from raw lithium minerals or chemicals. Lithium may be produced from brines, hard-rock spodumene, clays, or other sources, followed by concentration, chemical conversion, refining, and cell manufacturing. In the AMIP corpus, lithium is important mainly on the trade side and for critical-mineral supply-chain analysis rather than as a regular Arab mined commodity. It connects mineral trade with energy storage, electric mobility, and industrial policy. Arab trade and processing relevance: UAE, Saudi Arabia, Morocco, Egypt.

## Rare Earth Elements

Variant forms covered: rare earth elements.

Rare earth elements are a group of specialty metals used in permanent magnets, catalysts, polishing powders, batteries, phosphors, ceramics, defense systems, electronics, and renewable-energy technologies. They occur in minerals such as bastnaesite, monazite, xenotime, and ion-adsorption clays, and processing requires complex beneficiation, cracking, separation, and refining. Their economic importance comes from high-performance applications and difficult separation chemistry rather than bulk tonnage. In the AMIP knowledge base, rare earths are best treated as trade-side critical minerals unless a country-specific production record is present. They should not be confused with common industrial minerals. Arab trade and processing relevance: UAE, Saudi Arabia, Morocco, Egypt.

## Platinum Group Metals (PGMs)

Variant forms covered: platinum group metals and PGMs.

Platinum group metals are precious metals used in autocatalysts, chemical catalysts, hydrogen technologies, electronics, jewelry, medical devices, glass manufacturing, and high-temperature applications. They are commonly recovered from layered mafic intrusions, nickel-copper sulfide deposits, and recycling streams. Processing is technically demanding and involves concentration, smelting, converting, base-metal removal, and precious-metal refining. Their economic significance comes from high value, specialized uses, and sensitivity to industrial technology shifts. In AMIP, platinum group metals are included as trade-side critical minerals and should not be presented as a broad Arab mining production strength unless supported by a specific record. Arab trade and processing relevance: UAE, Saudi Arabia, Egypt, Morocco.

## Niobium

Variant forms covered: niobium and unwrought niobium, powders, waste, and scrap.

Niobium is a specialty metal used mainly to strengthen steels, improve high-strength low-alloy products, and support superalloys, superconductors, electronics, and advanced materials. It is mined from minerals such as pyrochlore and columbite, then concentrated, chemically processed, and converted into ferroniobium, oxides, metal, or other forms. Trade data may include unwrought metal, powders, waste, and scrap, which are downstream or recyclable forms rather than mine output. Niobium is economically significant because small additions can improve steel performance. In AMIP, it is mainly a trade-side critical mineral. Arab trade and processing relevance: UAE, Saudi Arabia, Egypt, Morocco.

## Vanadium

Variant forms covered: vanadium and vanadium with niobium where trade labels combine them.

Vanadium is used in steel alloys, titanium alloys, catalysts, chemicals, and vanadium redox flow batteries. It can be recovered from titaniferous magnetite, vanadiferous shales, slags, and other industrial residues; AMIP knowledge should keep the focus on mineral and trade classifications. Processing routes include concentration, roasting, leaching, precipitation, and conversion into oxides, ferrovanadium, or chemicals. Vanadium is economically important because it improves steel strength and has energy-storage relevance. In the corpus, combined trade labels with niobium should be mapped to both commodities for retrieval. Arab trade and processing relevance: UAE, Saudi Arabia, Egypt, Morocco.

## Gallium

Variant forms covered: gallium and combined gallium trade labels with vanadium, indium, niobium, and germanium.

Gallium is a specialty metal used in semiconductors, light-emitting diodes, radio-frequency devices, solar cells, magnets, and research materials. It is usually recovered as a by-product from bauxite processing or zinc residues rather than mined as a primary ore. Processing involves extraction from process liquors, purification, and conversion into high-purity metal or compounds. Gallium's economic significance comes from electronics and strategic technology uses, not from large-volume mining. In AMIP, gallium is primarily a trade-side critical mineral and should be linked to aluminium and zinc value chains only where appropriate. Arab trade and processing relevance: UAE, Saudi Arabia, Egypt, Morocco.

## Germanium

Variant forms covered: germanium and combined germanium trade labels with gallium, vanadium, indium, and niobium.

Germanium is a specialty metalloid used in fiber optics, infrared optics, solar cells, semiconductors, catalysts, and polymerization chemistry. It is commonly recovered as a by-product from zinc ores, coal ash, or other residues, so mine production is indirect and technically specialized. Processing includes residue collection, leaching, purification, precipitation, reduction, and zone refining or compound production for high-purity uses. Germanium has economic importance because it supports advanced optics and communication technologies. In AMIP, it belongs to trade-side critical-mineral coverage rather than regular Arab mine production. Arab trade and processing relevance: UAE, Saudi Arabia, Egypt, Morocco.

## Indium

Variant forms covered: indium and combined indium trade labels with gallium, vanadium, niobium, and germanium.

Indium is a soft specialty metal used in transparent conductive coatings, flat-panel displays, touchscreens, thin-film solar cells, solders, semiconductors, and low-melting alloys. It is usually recovered as a by-product of zinc refining and requires specialized hydrometallurgical separation and purification. Because indium is tied to by-product streams, its availability depends on zinc mining, refinery technology, and demand for high-purity compounds. In AMIP, indium should be treated as a trade-side critical mineral and connected to zinc value chains only where this helps explain supply. Arab trade and processing relevance: UAE, Saudi Arabia, Egypt, Morocco.

## Selenium, Tellurium, and Borates

Variant forms covered: selenium, tellurium, and borates.

Selenium and tellurium are specialty by-products often associated with copper refining and other metallurgical residues. Selenium is used in glass, pigments, metallurgy, electronics, and some agricultural or chemical applications; tellurium is used in solar cells, thermoelectrics, alloys, rubber, and specialty chemicals. Borates are boron minerals used in glass, ceramics, detergents, fertilizers, insulation, flame retardants, and specialty chemicals. These commodities are grouped here because trade labels may combine them, but they are chemically distinct and should be separated when data permits. In AMIP, they are mainly trade-side critical or specialty minerals rather than broad Arab mined commodities. Arab trade and processing relevance: UAE, Saudi Arabia, Egypt, Morocco.

## Zirconium

Variant forms covered: zirconium.

Zirconium is a corrosion-resistant metal derived mainly from zircon, a heavy mineral found in mineral sands. Zircon is used in ceramics, foundry sands, refractories, opacifiers, and chemicals, while zirconium metal and alloys are used in demanding industrial and energy applications. Mining involves heavy-mineral sand extraction and separation by gravity, magnetic, and electrostatic methods, followed by chemical conversion for higher-value products. Zirconium is economically significant because of its ceramic and high-performance materials markets. In AMIP, zirconium is a trade-side critical mineral unless a specific local heavy-mineral production record is present. Arab trade and processing relevance: UAE, Saudi Arabia, Egypt, Morocco.

## Silicon

Variant forms covered: silicon.

Silicon in trade and critical-mineral contexts usually means metallurgical silicon or silicon-based materials, not ordinary silica sand. It is used in aluminium alloys, silicones, semiconductors, solar cells, chemicals, and high-performance materials. Silicon metal is produced by reducing high-purity silica with carbon in electric furnaces, so it connects upstream quartz and silica resources with downstream energy-intensive processing. In AMIP retrieval, silicon should be distinguished from silica, quartz, and silica sand, although the supply chains are related. Its economic significance comes from electronics, solar manufacturing, aluminium, and chemical applications. Arab trade and processing relevance: UAE, Saudi Arabia, Egypt, Morocco, Oman.
